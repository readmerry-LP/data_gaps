📖 Presenter Cheat Sheet

🏠 Overview

Set the stage. Show the big picture before diving into specifics.
Total Gap EventsRaw count of missing or incorrect fields this week. One shipment can have several.
Unique DocketsHow many individual shipments have at least one open item — a cleaner measure of scope.
Warehouses AffectedHow spread out the issue is across the network this week.
Carried-Over Rate“This tells us how many open items were also open last week. Let’s talk about what’s getting in the way of clearing them.”
Weekly TrendShow direction of travel. Even a small downward trend is worth calling out as a win.
New vs. Carried-OverRed = still open from before, blue = new this week. “Our goal is to grow the blue and shrink the red.”
Gap Age Breakdown“The Chronic slice is where I most want to help — these have been open 7+ weeks and I want to understand what’s blocking them.”

🏭 Warehouses

Filter to their warehouse. Walk through each section together.
Gap Types chart“This is your specific breakdown — what’s flagging most often for you. Let’s talk about the top one or two.”
Weekly TrendPoint out any downward movement as a win. If flat, frame it as an opportunity.
Gap Age donut“The Chronic slice is what I want to prioritize with you — these are the ones we can likely close with one conversation.”
Top Org CodesHelps separate a warehouse process issue from a client data issue.
Action ItemsUse as conversation starters. “Does this match what you’re seeing? What would make this easier?”

📋 Gap Types

Network-wide view. Normalizes the problem — most warehouses share the same top issues.
Orders / Receipts / WW ETAsEach bar = one data field, ranked by frequency. “Missing POD is the #1 across the whole network — so you’re not alone in this.”

🔁 Recurrence

For conversations about specific long-open dockets. Lead with curiosity, not blame.
Persistent Dockets“These have been open 3+ weeks. I want to understand what’s keeping them open — is there something we can unblock on our end?”

🔥 Org Codes

Use to show whether a gap problem is local or comes from a specific client.
Heat mapDark across one row = client issue. Dark in one cell = local process. “This helps us point the conversation in the right direction.”

💡 Observations

Auto-written each week. Good for briefing a manager before a meeting.
Success StoriesAlways lead with this. “MNJ dropped 94% — whatever you did, it worked. What changed?”
Bottom LineOne-paragraph summary. Copy into Teams or email as a weekly manager briefing.
